About

I work as a CAD Engineer at onsemi, in a team which is responsible for EDA (Electronic Design Automation) tools support and custom tools development for all mixed-signal and digital design teams in the company. I typically write code which serves the needs of digital teams, PDK teams and Reticle Data Preparation teams. I graduated with a Bachelors degree in Electronics and Communications engineering in the year 2021. These are my early career years so I keep on learning something or the other every day about the chip development process and tools. As a CAD engineer, I get myself exposed to various things and I am expected to know many things although not in a deep manner. "Jack of all all trades, master of none" kind of summarizes the role I am currently in.

I spend my free time in the opensource domain. From the past couple of years, there is a huge traction for the opensource silicon. In short, one can typically design a chip, get it manufactured, packaged and ship it directly to his door steps. I think this is just the beginning of the opensource silicon and in future, the usage and capabilities of these initiatives will definitely increase by many times. In short, I feel this is the next opensource software movement which literally enabled everyone the freedom to write and publish softwares. I myself contribute to a few projects which directly supports the opensource IP development.
